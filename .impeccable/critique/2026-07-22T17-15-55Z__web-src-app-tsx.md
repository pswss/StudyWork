## Anti-patterns verdict

**LLM assessment:** Borderline pass. StudyWork is distinctive and cohesive, but dark editorial surfaces, acid lime, abstract chrome 3D, animated grain, tracked uppercase labels, and numbered tabs together form a recognizable 2026 “anti-SaaS AI” lane. The product feels intentionally art-directed, yet sometimes more like an awards concept than a study tool. The strongest AI tell is not the palette itself; it is full-row lime selection plus glow, repeated across an otherwise restrained interface.

**Deterministic scan:** 3 warnings in `web/src/styles.css`: one bounce-shaped loading animation (`aiBounce`) and two layout-property transitions (custom cursor width/height/margin and subject-card padding-left). Strict false positives: 0. The cursor and loading findings are mitigated by device and reduced-motion gates; the subject-card padding transition is an unqualified issue.

**Visual overlays:** Unavailable. The isolated browser runtime exposed no browser backend, so mutable injection and a user-visible overlay could not run. Existing screenshots were used only for stable visual-harmony evidence; their pre-HEAD LaTeX and mobile-overflow defects were excluded.

## Overall impression

Premium entrance, disciplined palette, and unusually complete feedback states create trust. The largest opportunity is to let the product surface replace showcase styling during focused study: reserve solid lime for primary action, restore familiar selection affordances, and sequence quiz setup.

## What is working

1. Persistent source feedback (`n/n selected`) is concise, immediate, and visible while the picker is collapsed.
2. Upload, background-job, quiz-progress, answer, retry, and recovery states are thorough and consistent.
3. Charcoal, bone, restrained geometry, and a single accent create strong cross-screen harmony without generic card grids or glass panels.

## Cognitive load

- Single focus: fail — quiz bank exposes starting, generation, printing, selection, and management together.
- Chunking: fail — the quiz control rows and six-tab navigation exceed four-item chunks.
- Grouping: pass — sidebar, tabs, source picker, file groups, and answer feedback are structurally clear.
- Visual hierarchy: pass — type and lime reliably identify primary state and action.
- One thing at a time: fail — strong in quiz play, weak in quiz setup.
- Minimal choices: fail — quiz setup and source lists present too many simultaneous decisions.
- Working memory: pass — selected counts, range, and progress remain visible.
- Progressive disclosure: pass — tabs, native details, file groups, and question expansion work well.

Result: high load (4/8 failures), concentrated in the quiz bank. Chat and quiz play are low-load.

## Emotional journey

The archive entrance feels premium and intriguing. Subject entry preserves confidence; chat and quiz play become focused and reassuring. Quiz configuration is the valley: dense controls, weak visible labels, and saturated selection styling make setup feel administrative. Immediate correctness feedback and retry-first results provide a strong recovery and ending.

## Priority issues

### [P1] Selection is color-heavy and ambiguous

- Why it matters: all-selected SourcePicker rows become a lime wall. Lime simultaneously means primary action, active navigation, selection, and success. Hidden checkboxes remove a familiar affordance and make state more color-dependent.
- Fix: show a visible check/indeterminate marker, use a subtle selected tint and border, remove glow from large selected rows, and retain the excellent count summary.
- Suggested command: `$impeccable polish`

### [P1] Critical controls lack persistent labels or accessible names

- Why it matters: repeated quiz number fields and selects depend on position or placeholders. The send arrow and quiz delete icon are unclear to assistive technology; question expansion is a click-only span.
- Fix: visibly separate “Quiz conditions” from “Generate questions,” label counts, name icon buttons, and make expandable question text a real button.
- Suggested command: `$impeccable clarify`

### [P1] Touch and keyboard boundaries are incomplete

- Why it matters: mode chips, 20px question selectors, and roughly 25–34px destructive actions miss the 44px coarse-pointer target. Subject rows are pointer-first.
- Fix: enlarge coarse-pointer hit areas, add `aria-pressed`, and make subject rows keyboard operable without changing their visual design.
- Suggested command: `$impeccable audit`

### [P2] Quiz setup lacks a decision sequence

- Why it matters: range, source, difficulty, count, wrong-only, generation, printing, and management compete in one layer.
- Fix: create two plain product groups: “Quiz conditions” and “Generate questions.” Keep generation visible for now, but visually subordinate it.
- Suggested command: `$impeccable layout`

### [P2] Decorative motion spends attention and layout work

- Why it matters: subject-card padding animation reflows a grid; custom cursor geometry animates layout properties; loading dots bounce. Animated grain adds constant paint without aiding study.
- Fix: remove the card reflow and grain loop, use transform for cursor geometry, and use opacity rather than vertical bounce for pending dots.
- Suggested command: `$impeccable animate`

## Persona red flags

**Alex (power user):** Bulk selection, printing, Enter, Escape, and arrow-key tabs help. Click-only subject/question rows and missing answer-choice accelerators interrupt keyboard flow.

**Sam (accessibility-dependent):** Semantic tabs and focus rings are strong. Hidden checkbox visuals, color-heavy selection, undersized targets, low-contrast placeholders, and unlabeled icons obstruct use.

**Casey (mobile):** Main content correctly precedes the sidebar. Small chips and delete controls, horizontally scrolling tabs without an overflow cue, and dense quiz setup are difficult one-handed.

## Minor observations

- `--ink-3` is near the AA boundary for 9.5–12px text; `--ink-4` placeholders are substantially below it.
- “OBSIDIAN ARCHIVE” and raw AI runtime details serve the maker more than the learner.
- Numbered tabs imply sequence although the tabs are peer destinations.
- Native select popups need an explicit dark `color-scheme` to preserve harmony across browsers.
- AI-generation source selection currently resets when ready-material membership changes; it should use the same exclusion-set contract as chat and notes.

## Questions to consider

1. Should solid lime mean only primary action, with selection shown by checks and a restrained tint?
2. Should question generation become collapsed progressive disclosure after the first polish pass?
3. Should 3D/archive identity remain equally prominent inside focused study screens?
